Game Idea: XO Advanced

General Description:

The game is a smart and fun development of the classic XO game.

It is played on a large board (a big XO) divided into 9 squares, each of which contains a small XO game on its own.

How to Play?

1. The first player (X) chooses any of the 9 squares (any small XO) and places their mark (X) in one of the cells inside it.

2. The second player (O) doesn't get to choose freely where to play, but:

The small square (small XO) where the first player placed their mark determines the next big square to play in.

Example: If X plays in the top-right cell of a small XO, player O must play in the top-right big XO.

3. If the square they are supposed to play in is closed (already won or tied), the player is free to choose any small XO they want.

4. The Goal:

Win a small XO by placing 3 consecutive marks (X or O).

When you win a small XO, it counts as if you have claimed a cell in the big XO.

To win the game, you need to claim 3 consecutive small XOs (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally) in the big XO.

Main Features of the Game:

Strategic thinking: You need to plan not only where to place your mark but also where to send your opponent to play.

The game teaches forward planning and control over the game's flow.

Interactive design that is simple yet highly challenging.

You can play against a friend or against AI.

Example for Clarification:

X played in the middle small XO and chose the left cell.

O must now play in the big XO on the left.

O placed their mark in the left big XO, in the top-right cell.

Now X must play in the big XO at the top-right... and so on.
